# Terminology Policy

## Precedence

Choose terms in this order:

1. Matter-specific terms expressly approved by the responsible reviewer.
2. The filed translation or controlling same-family filing designated by the user.
3. Client style guide and maintained translation memory.
4. A current official patent-office glossary or WIPO Pearl validated term.
5. A reliable domain source written by subject-matter experts.
6. A proposed translation marked [PROPOSED].

Higher precedence does not cure an obvious scope error. Record the conflict and route it to the reviewer.

## Termbase fields

Each entry contains:

- concept_id;
- source_term;
- target_term;
- subject_field;
- definition or context;
- source and pinpoint;
- reliability;
- status: approved, provisional, deprecated, or forbidden;
- permitted variants;
- forbidden variants;
- reviewer and date.

## Selection rules

- One concept receives one preferred target term.
- One target term maps to one concept where practical; homonyms require definitions.
- Defined terms preserve capitalization and quotation treatment.
- Abbreviations are introduced once and used consistently.
- Transliteration, translation, and retained source-language text are deliberate choices recorded in the termbase.
- A WIPO Pearl machine suggestion remains provisional unless its reliability and context support it.

## Updating

Never rewrite historical approvals. Deprecate an entry, add its replacement, and record the effective date and affected documents.
