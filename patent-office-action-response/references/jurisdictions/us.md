# USPTO Module

Use the current MPEP revision and controlling statutes, regulations, and precedent. MPEP guidance assists analysis but does not replace the underlying law.

## Record fields

- Office Action date, reply period exactly as stated, art unit, examiner, application number, and final or non-final posture.
- Every rejection, objection, requirement, allowable claim, and examiner indication.
- Current claim status and amendment history.

## Routing

- Section 101: use the current eligibility guidance and record the specific eligibility analysis.
- Section 102: map every claim limitation to a single asserted reference and pinpoint.
- Section 103: map references, missing limitations, articulated rationale, and evidence.
- Section 112(a): separate written description and enablement.
- Section 112(b): identify the exact indefinite term or relationship and the examiner's interpretation.
- Section 112(f): identify invoked functional language and corresponding disclosed structure where relevant.
- Restriction, election, double patenting, formality, and drawing objections receive separate inventory rows.

## Replies and amendments

Use MPEP 714 and the current regulations to determine presentation and responsiveness. Distinguish non-final and final practice before proposing an amendment, interview, continued examination, appeal, or another procedural route.

Every amended claim limitation receives:

- marked change;
- clean claim text;
- as-filed support pinpoint;
- written-description analysis;
- effect on dependent claims;
- reason it addresses a specific rejection.

MPEP 2163.06 distinguishes new matter added to the disclosure from unsupported matter added to claims. Keep that distinction explicit.

## Definiteness

Under MPEP 2173, evaluate terms in context and avoid per se treatment. Relative or functional language is not automatically indefinite. Record the examiner's stated reason the claim boundary is unclear and respond with specification context, evidence of skilled-person understanding, or a supported amendment.

## Record and interview

An interview plan identifies the proposal and fallback. The substance and any agreed action are made of record using the current USPTO procedure.
