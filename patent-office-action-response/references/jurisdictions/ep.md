# EPO Module

Use the current annual EPO Guidelines edition. The April 2026 edition is the baseline at package build time and superseded April 2025.

## Record fields

- Communication type and EPC or Rule basis.
- Date, time limit exactly as stated, examining division, application number, and pending requests.
- Every objection, affected claim, reference, and requested action.

## Routing

- Art. 54: novelty against one disclosure.
- Art. 56: inventive step using the problem-solution approach.
- Art. 83: sufficiency.
- Art. 84: clarity and support.
- Art. 123(2): added subject matter.
- Rule 137: admissibility and identification of amendments and their basis.
- Unity, formal requirements, description adaptation, and procedural issues receive separate rows.

## Art. 123(2) basis

For every amendment, identify the application-as-filed basis and explain why the skilled person derives the amended subject matter directly and unambiguously, using common general knowledge and considering explicit and implicit disclosure. A list of isolated pinpoints is not enough when the amendment combines features from different embodiments.

## Inventive step

Follow the current problem-solution approach:

1. determine the closest prior art;
2. establish the objective technical problem based on distinguishing features and effects;
3. assess whether the skilled person would have arrived at the claimed solution.

Keep could-would reasoning, technical and non-technical features, combination of references, hindsight, and evidence in separate fields.

## Reply and interaction

Address all objections even though a reply may be procedurally recognized when incomplete. Use consultation or oral proceedings terminology and procedure as currently applicable; do not import USPTO interview or RCE concepts.
